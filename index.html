Questions PTOs Always Ask

Apex earns its share of the gross raised — between 40% and 48% depending on how much your school raises, per the official price scale shown in the comparison table above. There's no hidden fee, no per-student charge, and no markup on materials. The more your school raises, the smaller Apex's percentage becomes. That alignment — Apex only earns more when your school earns more — is intentional.
No long-term contract is required. Each event is a single engagement. Many schools do choose to bring Apex back year after year — you saw several repeat schools in the results above — but that's always their choice, not a contractual obligation. You're never locked in.
First-time schools are a specialty. Racine Montessori (114 students, first-time Apex school) raised $21,871 — a 68% participation rate with 8.14 shares per student. Kaneland John Shields ran their first Apex event and raised $43,043 with 421 students. The Apex on-campus team handles all setup, student energy, and event-day logistics — your PTO's job is simply to say yes and communicate with families.
Significantly less than a DIY event. Apex provides a dedicated on-campus team that handles classroom lessons, morning arrival energy, event-day setup, the run itself, and full teardown. Your PTO's primary responsibilities are communicating the fundraiser to families (Apex provides templates), distributing pledge kits, and collecting donations — all of which Apex walks you through step by step.
A typical Apex event runs across one school week. Monday through Thursday, the Apex team visits classrooms to deliver leadership lessons and build excitement — each day has a theme. Friday is event day: the fun run itself, with Apex handling all logistics from setup through teardown. Prizes are distributed to students in the days following based on pledges collected. Dean can walk you through a school-specific timeline when you reach out.
